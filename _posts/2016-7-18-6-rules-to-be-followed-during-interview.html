--- layout: post title: 6 Rules For Answering Interviews That I Learnt ---

A shear mention of word “interview” can unnerve a fresher. Everyone of us has gone through this phase at some point in our lives. Preparing for an interview without proper research in itself can be a bit confusing and on the day of the acid-test, it can get you in hot water. Companies need an exact candidate who suits their values, work culture and requirements, and not preparing for it can lead to irreversible goof-ups.


Source: https://pixabay.com/en/interview-job-icon-job-interview-1018332/

Recently I wrote an article regarding the experiences of Barclays interview on CSEStack. CSEStack collects experiences of the candidates appearing for various organisations including Persistent, IBM, Amazon, Intelligence Bureau etc. I have described the procedure that I went through during placement drive in detail in that article. Hence instead of repeating it here, I would focus more on how I think you should present yourself during interview.


I compiled list of 6 rules from my experence and my friends' interview stories as to how to present yourself at the interview.

Rule 1: Know Your Recruiter

Know the vision of the company you are applying for. It helps you explain why you are the candidate they are searching (They did ask me this.). Have a look at the history of the company. In my case it was Barclays, who are pioneers in almost any banking technology that we use today. With such a long history of 300+ years, it is not otherworldly if they expect you to know about the company.

Rule 2: Write Down Your Aim

What do you see yourself doing at the end of the placement process? What are your aims? Where do you see yourself after 4 years? How do you expect the work environment in the company? Be clear about your perspective towards job. Write it down in advance so that you don't fumble during interview. Corporate leadership loves candidates with clear thoughts.

Rule 3: Revise Your Resume

I can't get tired emphasizing this. Have a professional resume. Trim it and format it. Proofread it. There is nothing worse than interviewer pointing at errors in the resume during interview. See if you have provided contact details. Have you updated your GPA? Put your resume through meticulous scan and update it when necessary. You may have a look at my resume. It, by not means is, perfect or not even ideal, but it has all the elements to be included in a professional resume and it is definitely gives you an idea to begin with.

Rule 4: Don't Answer, Have a Good Conversation

One of the motives behind conducting an interview is to know you as a person. Given short period of time, it is something that you can only prove through good communication. Gone are the days when interview was analogous to oral examination. Interview in the form of conversation not only gives you enough room to relax but also leaves a lasting impression on the interviewer.

Rule 5: Don't Miss Opportunity To Learn

Interviewer will give you an opportunity to ask question. Make best of it! This is the time to learn about the company you would be soon joining. Also, during interview, interviewer might explain you a concept. Make sure you understand it very well. Ask doubts until you are satisfied, because next question might be related to it.

Rule 6: Do Not Lie

You are not expected to know everything. Nor you are expected to have superhuman past record. Be who you are and speak the truth. Do not brag about the skills that you do not possess. Do not produce stories in the air. I was asked about my role model, and I frankly and truthfully told them I did not have any. Interviewers are trained and they spot the lies in an instance. It is definitely not worth.


At the end of the day, I am not an expert and this is definitely not an exclusive list of all To-Dos during interview. But these are the points that I have learnt either from my own experience the hard way or through others' experience.


All the best!!
